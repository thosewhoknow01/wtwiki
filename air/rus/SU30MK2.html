Overview

The Su-30 Mk2 AMV is a two-seat, twin-engine multirole fighter derived from the Su-30 family. It combines long-range strike capability with air-to-air performance and advanced avionics, making it a flexible platform for both air superiority and ground attack missions. Avionics & Sensors

The Su-30 Mk2 AMV is fitted with a multi-mode pulse-Doppler radar capable of long-range target detection, tracking multiple targets and guiding semi-active and active missiles. An IRST (infrared search and track) system provides passive detection of heat-emitting targets. The aircraft integrates an electronic warfare (EW) suite for self-protection and countermeasures, and supports targeting pods for precision-guided munitions.

Weapons & Loadouts

Typical loadouts include a mix of short- and medium-range air-to-air missiles, anti-ship and anti-radiation missiles, guided bombs, and unguided rockets. The internal 30 mm cannon provides close-range engagement capability. Below are detailed missile statistics for common weapons carried by Su-30 variants.

Typical mission loadouts

Air superiority: 4× R-77 (underwing/pylon), 2× R-73 (wingtip), internal cannon; ECM pod optional.

Strike / Interdiction: 2× Kh-31 (stand-off), 2× Kh-29 or 4× KAB-500, targeting pod, external fuel tanks for extended range.

Maritime strike: 2× Kh-31A (anti-ship), 2× Kh-35 (if available), anti-ship targeting pod, chaff/flare dispensers.

Tactics and employment notes

  • Use R-77s for beyond-visual-range engagements; combine with AWACS or datalink for extended detection.
  • Employ R-73s in short-range, high-off-boresight engagements with helmet-mounted cueing for quick target acquisition.
  • For SEAD, pair Kh-31P (anti-radiation) with stand-off tactics to suppress enemy radars before strike packages enter contested airspace.
  • Use targeting pods and KABs for precision strikes to minimize collateral damage and increase mission success against hardened targets.

Maintenance & logistics

Field maintenance requires trained avionics technicians and access to spare modules for radar, flight control computers, and engines. Missile integration requires certified pylons and wiring harnesses; guided munitions need pre-flight checks, seeker boresight, and mission planning uploads. Warhead handling must follow ordnance safety protocols.
