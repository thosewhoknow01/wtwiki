Real-world history

The Leopard 2A7V is Germany’s latest comprehensive upgrade of the Leopard 2 series. In 2017, the Bundeswehr contracted Krauss-Maffei Wegmann (KMW) to upgrade existing vehicles and produce additional units, with deliveries beginning around 2020 and completing in subsequent years. The 2A7V package emphasizes protection, mobility, endurance, and firepower, pairing the 120 mm L/55 A1 gun with improved thermal imaging and a digitized fire-control suite.

Beyond armor and systems integration, the modernization includes survivability and sustainment improvements for sustained operations, reflecting lessons learned from deployments and NATO standardization goals.

War Thunder gameplay

At Rank VIII and BR 12.7 across all modes, Leopard 2A7V is a top-tier MBT known for its gun handling, optics, and reliable stabilization. It excels in long-range engagements and hull-down fighting, leveraging thermals and a strong ready-rack to maintain pressure.

Strengths include the Rh120 L/55 A1 cannon with high-penetration APFSDS, refined commander/gunner sights, and quick turret/gun drives. Weaknesses include vulnerability to hull ammo and average raw acceleration compared to some peers; many players mitigate risk by carrying fewer rounds.

Tactics: Anchor on ridgelines, use smoke proactively, and trade with precision. On open maps, play as a sniper with laser rangefinder and thermals; in urban maps, minimize profile and exploit reverse speed to break contact.

Manufacturer — Krauss-Maffei Wegmann (KMW) / KNDS Deutschland

Krauss-Maffei Wegmann (KMW) originated in 1999 from the merger of Krauss-Maffei’s defense division and Wegmann & Co. Headquartered in Munich, KMW has produced cornerstone European systems such as the Leopard 2 family and the PzH 2000. In 2015, KMW and France’s Nexter formed the joint holding KNDS; the German branch later adopted the name KNDS Deutschland.

The firm’s lineage reaches back to the 19th century: Maffei (founded in 1838) developed locomotives and heavy engineering capabilities, while Wegmann (since 1886, Kassel) progressed from rolling stock to armored vehicle technology. Their combined heritage shaped KMW’s modern competency in complex land systems, integration, and sustainment — culminating in the Leopard 2A7V modernization.
