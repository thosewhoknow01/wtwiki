Real-world history

The T-80BVM is a modernization of the Soviet-era T-80BV, developed to extend service life and improve survivability and lethality. Upgrades include the Relikt explosive reactive armor (ERA), an improved fire control system, modern optics and thermal sights, and sustainment improvements. The BVM program began in the late 2010s to bring existing T-80 fleets closer to contemporary battlefield requirements.

The T-80 family traces back to the 1970s as a gas-turbine powered MBT designed for high mobility. The BVM modernization reflects incremental improvements in protection and electronics rather than a complete redesign.

War Thunder gameplay

In War Thunder the T-80BVM occupies a top-tier role where mobility, ERA protection, and a capable 125 mm gun combine to create a versatile MBT. It performs well as a fast flanker, can trade shots when hull-down, and uses guided missiles to threaten distant or well-armored targets.

Strengths: Excellent acceleration and top speed for its class; Relikt ERA reduces the effectiveness of many incoming rounds; modern FCS and thermals improve first-shot probability.

Weaknesses: Turret and hull ammo storage remain a vulnerability; close-quarters fights and flanking by lighter vehicles can be dangerous. The gas turbine engine is powerful but can be more maintenance intensive in roleplay terms.

Tactics: Use speed to control engagement distance, exploit hull-down positions, and employ guided missiles against distant MBTs or to finish damaged targets. Avoid prolonged exposure in open terrain without support; use smoke and terrain to reset engagements when necessary.

Manufacturer — Omsktransmash / Kirov

The T-80 series was produced by several Soviet and Russian factories, notably the Leningrad Kirov Plant and Omsktransmash. These manufacturers have a long history of armored vehicle production and modernization programs, supporting upgrades such as the BVM package through integration of ERA, electronics, and sustainment systems.
